Class VII Strong Verbs

Class VII verbs are the most irregular. They can have an (a/o/ea/æ/e + consonant). For example, 'hatan - to order', 'rædan - to counsel', 'hleapan - to leap' or 'growan - to grow'. When they conjugate, they can follow one of two patterns. Either they take 'e' in both the past singular and past plural or they take 'eo'. Examine the verbs 'hatan - to order' and 'cnawan - to know' in the sentences below.

When voiceless stops appear in a voiced environment (not initially, finally or next to other voiceless consonants) and are not immediately proceeded by the stress, they become a voiced stop. So p becomes b; t, þ and ð become d; k becomes g; and s becomes r.

When voiceless stops appear in a voiced environment (not initially, finally or next to other voiceless consonants) and are not immediately proceeded by the stress, they become a voiced stop. So p becomes b; t, þ and ð become d; k becomes g; and s becomes r.

Because of the wide range of verbs in this class, it is the most commonly encountered. Many of the words should be recognisable to an English speaker, and they have kept their vowel change though usually it's been simplified to a single 'e'. For example, 'grow' and 'grew', 'leap' and 'lept', 'know' and 'knew' or 'fall' and 'fell'. You can practice Class VII verbs below.

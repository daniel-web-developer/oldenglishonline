Class VI Strong Verbs

Class VI verbs have two forms. The first form has an (a + consonant). For example, 'dragan - to draw' or 'spanan - to seduce'. When they conjugate, they follow the vowel change pattern a - o - o. So the root vowel in the present is 'a', the root vowel in the past singular is 'o', and the root vowel in the past plural is 'o'. For example, 'ic draga - I draw', becomes 'ic drog - I said' or 'hie drogon - they said'. Examine the verb 'wascan - to wash' in the sentences below.

The second form has an (ie + double consonant) or an (e + double consonant). For example, 'hliehhan - to laugh' or 'steppan - to step'. The conjugation pattern remains the same with the past singular and past plural changing the 'ie' to 'o'. For example, 'ic stepe - I step' becomes 'heo stop - she stepped' and 'hie stopon - they stepped'. Examine the verb 'swerian' in the sentences below.

When voiceless stops appear in a voiced environment (not initially, finally or next to other voiceless consonants) and are not immediately proceeded by the stress, they become a voiced stop. So p becomes b; t, þ and ð become d; k becomes g; and s becomes r.

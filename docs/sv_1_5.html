A Quick Note on Pronounciation

You will notice that some letters in the following module are marked with a macron (¯) above the letter. This is to signify they are long vowels and so sound different to how the vowel is normally pronounced. These macrons are never present in manuscripts and are simply a guide for you to differentiate the sounds.

Class Root Signature Example Change Pattern
I i + consonant Writan ī - ā - i
II eo/u + consonant Beodan, Brucan ēo/u - ēa - u
III vowel + l/r/h/n + consonant Helpan, Weorpan, Feohtan, Findan i - a - u, eo/e - ea - u
IV e + l/r/m Beran, Stelan e - æ - ǣ
V e + consonant (not l/r/m/n) Sprecan e - æ - ǣ
VI a + consonant or e/ie + double consnant Wascan, Hliehhan a - o - ō
VII a/o/ea/æ/e + consonant Hatan, Rædan a - e - ē, ea - eo - ēo

Conjunctions

Conjunctions are words that join two words, phrases, clauses or sentences. In modern English, these are words like 'and', 'but', or 'because'. Conjunctions function exactly the same in Old English as they do in modern English, and many of the words have stayed very simliar as the langauge has evolved. For example, 'ond - and', 'to - to', or 'æfter - after'. However, there are a few false friends you should be careful of.

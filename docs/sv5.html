Class IV Strong Verbs

Class IV verbs have a (vowel + l/r). For example, 'beran - to bear' or 'stelan - to steal'. When they conjugate, they follow the vowel change pattern e - æ - ǣ. So the root vowel in the present is 'e', the root vowel in the past singular is 'æ', and the root vowel in the past pural is 'ǣ'. For example, 'ic cwele - I die', becomes 'ic cwæl - I died' or 'hie cwælon - they died'. Examine the verb 'beran - to bear' in the sentence below.

There are two verbs which are considered Class IV verbs but which are irregular: 'niman - to take' and 'cuman - to come'. These are two of the most commonly used verbs so it is important to recognise them in all their forms. The vowel in both verbs becomes 'o' in the past singular and 'o' in the past plural.

  • Niman

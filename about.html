What is this?

Learning Old English Online was originally designed and developed by Victoria Koivisto-Kokko at University College Cork as part of her Master’s thesis. The primary aim of her research was to test cognitive theories of learning online and ascertain the optimial design for an online course aimed at teaching medieval languages. Using Old English as the core subject, a course design and environmental design was researched using a combination of faculty interviews, persona testing, and research on cognitive theories of learning, and this website was built and tested.

Is it still being developed?

Yes! While, the Master's is long over, the passion for Old English and medieval languages in general remains, so the project continues to be developed and streamlined.

How does it work?

The website is structured so that major grammatical concepts are covered in an individual module, which is subdivided into topics. Topics contain textual information accompanied by graphic organisers and interactive fill-in-the-blank questions which provide explanatory feedback. The enviroment is specifically designed to require the minumum amount of cognitive processing.

How much content is there?

A list of the modules can be found on the Index page. Currently, only the modules ‘Cases and Strong Masculine Nouns’ and 'Weak Verbs' are populated. However, this project is still in development with new modules being added as soon as they are completed.

Where else can I find stuff?

For those interested in additional learning resources, you can find a comprehensive overview of Old English resources on the website of the Teachers of Old English in Britain and Ireland: http://www.toebi.org.uk/resources/

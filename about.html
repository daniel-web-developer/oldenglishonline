About

Learning Old English Online was designed and developed by Victoria Koivisto-Kokko at University College Cork as part of her Master’s thesis. The primary aim of her research was to ascertain the best design for an online course aimed at teaching medieval languages. Using Old English as the core subject, a course design and environmental design was researched using a combination of persona testing and research on cognitive theories of learning. The course was built as a website, and tested via group comparison testing.

The website is structured so that major grammatical concepts are covered in an individual module, which is subdivided into topics. Topics contain textual information accompanied by graphical organisers and interactive fill-in-the-blank questions which provide explanatory feedback.

A list of the modules can be found on the Index page. Currently, only the module ‘Cases and Strong Masculine Nouns’ is populated. However, after the completion of the MA, the project will continue to be expanded both in terms of the underlying technical framework and the content.

For those interested in additional learning resources, you can find a comprehensive overview of Old English resources on the website of the Teachers of Old English in Britain and Ireland: http://www.toebi.org.uk/resources/

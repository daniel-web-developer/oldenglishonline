Prehistory

Proto Indo European is the linguistic reconstruction of the common ancestor of all Indo-European languages. PIE is estimated to have been spoken as a single language from 4500 BC to 2500 BC and all Celtic, Germanic, Hellenic, Indo-Iranian and Romance languages are descended from it.

7th Century

Cædmon's Hymn is a short poem composed by Cædmon, an illiterate cow-herd employed by the monastery of Whitby. It was composed sometime between 658 and 680 and is the oldest surviving record of Old English, being recorded in the early part of the 8th century and surviving in 19 verified manuscript witness.

8th Century

The Ruthwell Cross is a stone cross probably dating from the 7th or 8th century, when the village of Ruthwell, Scotland, was part of the Anglo-Saxon Kingdom of Northumbria. A runic inscription surrounds the carving on one side of the cross, though it is widely believed these runes were added later, possibly as late as the 10th century.

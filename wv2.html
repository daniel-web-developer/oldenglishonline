Personal Pronouns

Before we begin exploring verbs, there's a few things you need to know. If you want to look up a verb in a dictionary, you need to use the infinitive of the verb. In Modern English this would be 'to love', 'to go', 'to drink'. In Old English, the infinitive generally ends in 'an': hieran - to hear, feran - to go, lufian - to love. Verbs conjugate through a mixture of inflectional suffixes or stem-modifications, which will be explained in the coming topics.

However, before we start exploring verb conjugation tables, it is necessary to explore personal pronouns. Personal pronouns take different forms depending on number, case, grammatical gender, and formality.

Personal Pronouns
Old English Modern English
1st Person Singular Ic I
2nd Person Singular Þu You
3rd Person Singular He / Heo / Hit He / She / It
1st Person Plural We We
2nd Person Plural Ge Ye / You
3rd Person Plural Hie They

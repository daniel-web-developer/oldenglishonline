Weak Verbs II

Class II weak verbs have an infinitive ending in -ian, and are similar in form to Class I verbs. For example, lufian - to love loses its 'i' in the same conjugations where hieran loses its second 'e'. Examine the sentence below: 'He hears that she loves you'.

It should be noted that most verbs ending in -rian belong not to Class II but to Class I. The exceptions to this are andswarian - to answer, gadrian - to gather, and timbrian - to build, which conjugate the same as other Class II verbs.
